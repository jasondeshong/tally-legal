Tally Privacy Policy

Effective July 3, 2026 · Tally is built and operated by Jay DeShong.

Tally is a personal accountability app. The short version: your data is yours, it exists so the app can work for you, and nobody else can see it — not advertisers, not other users, not analytics companies. There are no trackers in Tally.

What Tally stores

  • Account — your email address and a display name you choose.
  • Habit data — your daily goals, check-offs, streaks, to-dos, board notes, books and reading logs, project timer minutes.
  • Energy check-ins — the wellness levels and optional notes you log. This is health-adjacent data and is treated as sensitive.
  • Voice transcripts — when you use the mic, the words you said are processed to update your data. Transcripts are not stored; the resulting actions (e.g. "completed Meditate") are.
  • AI usage metering — token counts per AI request, so usage and cost stay visible.

Who processes it

  • Supabase (hosting, database, authentication — servers in the United States). Every row is protected by row-level security: only your signed-in account can read your data.
  • Anthropic (AI processing). Two features send data to Anthropic's Claude API: voice logging sends your spoken transcript plus your goal and book names; the weekly Coach reflection sends a compact summary of your last seven days. Anthropic does not train models on API data. You're asked for consent before the first AI feature runs.

That's the whole list. Nothing is sold, shared, or used for advertising.

The Circle

If you connect with friends, they see exactly three things: your display name, today's completion ring, and your streak. Never your goals, notes, books, moods, or anything else.

Your controls

  • Export — download everything Tally holds about you as JSON, any time, from Settings.
  • Delete — permanently delete your account and every row of your data from Settings. This is immediate and irreversible.

Retention

Data is kept until you delete it. Deleting your account removes it all.
